PlanktoSpace: User Guide & Operational Manual

Synchronizing in-situ sampling with satellite overpasses.

PlanktoSpace is a decision-support tool designed for marine researchers. It solves a specific high-cost problem: ensuring that expensive water sampling occurs exactly when an ocean-colour satellite (Sentinel-3, PACE, Sentinel-2) is overhead, provided the sky is clear and the sea state allows for operation.

1. The Dashboard & Map

Upon launching, you are presented with the operational map. This interface is designed to be lightweight and responsive, even on older devices used in the field.

  • Select Location: Tap anywhere on the map or use the "My Location" button to snap to your vessel's GPS coordinates.
  • Search Criteria: The app defaults to a 2-day window. Use the "Refine Search" button to adjust:
    • Time Window: Filter passes to your working hours (e.g., 08:00 - 16:00 Local Time).
    • Chlorophyll Data: Toggle "Include Chlorophyll" Off for rapid geometric checks, or On to fetch the latest NOAA VIIRS data (requires ~20s processing time).
Engineering Note: The map engine is built on a custom Leaflet implementation optimized for offline caching. Once a location is queried, the data is persisted locally, allowing you to review results even if you lose signal at sea.

2. Analyzing Opportunities

Once you tap "Check Satellites", the system performs a real-time fusion of orbital data and weather forecasting.

We don't just list times; we rank them. The proprietary scoring algorithm weights four factors:

  • Cloud Cover (40%): Derived from multiple sources (Open-Meteo, MET Norway).
  • Elevation Angle (30%): Higher elevation means less atmospheric interference.
  • Logistics (15%): Distance from your vessel to the satellite's nadir.
  • Science (15%): Historical chlorophyll-a presence (is there anything to see?).

Source Confidence Indicators

PlanktoSpace uses a Hybrid Prediction Engine. We cross-reference API predictions (N2YO) with our own local SGP4 orbital propagator (Skyfield) using fresh TLE data.

3. Pass Details & Context

Tapping a pass reveals the full scientific context required for mission planning.

  • Geometry: View the exact Max Elevation, Azimuth, and Off-Nadir Angle.
  • Environmental Context:
    • Wind & Waves: Vital for deployment safety.
    • Cloud Sources: We compare multiple weather models to reduce uncertainty.
  • External Verification:
    • Windy.com: Deep link directly to the specific location/time layer.
    • NASA Worldview: Jump to the specific satellite pass visual to check historical trends.

4. Managing Data

Field campaigns are dynamic. PlanktoSpace allows you to curate your schedule.

  • Saving: Tap the Star Icon to save a pass to your local device database.
  • Offline Access: Saved passes are available without an internet connection.
  • Export: In the "Saved" tab, you can select multiple passes and "Copy as JSON". This allows you to paste the exact orbital parameters into your cruise reports or Python/R analysis scripts.
Privacy by Design: This application operates on an anonymous, serverless architecture. Your location data is processed ephemerally for the calculation and is never tracked or sold.

5. Troubleshooting & FAQ

Q: Why do I see a difference between this app and other trackers?
A: Most trackers show "Visual Passes" (when the satellite reflects sunlight at night). PlanktoSpace calculates Geometric/Radio Passes specifically for daytime optical sensing. Furthermore, we account for the specific Swath Width of instruments like OLCI and MSI, ensuring the pass is actually usable for your sensor.

Q: What if I have no internet?
A: If you have previously searched for a location, the data is cached. If you are moving into a dead zone, we recommend running a search for your target coordinates while you still have connectivity.
